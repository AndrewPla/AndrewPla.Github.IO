I'm a technical educator, community builder, and the guy who genuinely gets more energy from helping others level up than almost anything else.

By day I'm a Community Manager at PDQ, working alongside sysadmins and IT pros who remind me every single day why I got into this field. I'm also a Microsoft PowerShell MVP, podcast host, speaker, and Community Director of PowerShell Summit.

But here's the thing — the community stuff isn't just my job. It's my life. I show up to it the same way on a Tuesday at 11pm in a Discord channel as I do on a conference stage. It's not work when you genuinely believe in the people you're building with.

Every week I host a live podcast and stream on YouTube covering PowerShell, automation, and the humans behind the keyboards. Not just the technical stuff — the career journeys, the mindset shifts, the moments where something finally clicks.

I believe this community isn't just kind. It's wise. We understand that when we invest in each other, everyone wins. That's not charity. That's clarity.

If you're somewhere on your IT journey and you need someone in your corner, you're in the right place.
